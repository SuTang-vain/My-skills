# Source Validation

Use this file when a knowledge card includes facts that should be checked rather than inferred.

## When To Search

Search the web when the card mentions:

- current market size, salaries, pricing, or growth rates
- dated events or timelines
- company, product, policy, or regulation facts
- named reports, papers, surveys, or institutions
- direct claims that would be stronger with a source link

## Source Priority

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Original paper, dataset, filing, official documentation, official announcement
2. Official organization summary or publication page
3. High-quality reporting that directly cites the primary source
4. Commentary or secondary blog posts

## Output Behavior

- Put only the sources you actually relied on into `## 参考`.
- If a specific statement is uncertain, either soften the wording or say the claim remains unverified.
- Convert vague time references into exact dates when presenting the note.
- Do not fabricate citations to make the card look complete.

## Card-Friendly Referencing

- Keep references short.
- Prefer 2-6 strong links over a long noisy list.
- Choose links that future-you can trust and revisit quickly.
